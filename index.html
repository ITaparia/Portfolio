Easy Sleep (Pending Release)

Easy Sleep is an application that was mostly developed by me, but still released under the name of TopBox Software. I decided to make this app by myself mostly because I wanted to see the entire development process experienced by a sole person, to see how indie developers without huge corporations backing them built apps.

It was inspired by my, and the majority of the American teenage population having a lack of sleep. It uses the scientific basis of 90 minute sleep cycles to determine the optimal time for someone to either sleep at, or wake up.

Who am I?

Hello! I'm Ishan Taparia, and I attend William Fremd High School, in Inverness, IL. Just for a basic bio, I was born in India, and moved to Chicagoland at around 2004. Ever since then, I have lived here, and havent really moved around much.

Anything computer science related has always been a passion of mine, and I was drawn most to programming because of the infinite amount of opportunities it holds. To me, coding is almost like a form of art - it's the same thing as picking up a pencil, and drawing whatever you want, and it's the same thing as picking up a camera and shooting something. Just with a few lines of code, I am given the ability to create almost anything I want, and make it available to millions around the world.







What do I do?

Since I'm a highschool student, my schedule is usually very busy, with schoolwork taking up most of my day. Obviously, in any spare time I get, I love to code. In addition to coding, something I do to just relax for a bit is play the guitar, or maybe fire up a video game to play with friends.

In addition to schoolwork, I am on my school's debate team, and participate in National and State tournaments with my team. Debate Team has probably provided most of my learning experiences, and will most definitely help me in the future. In addition to debating, I also like to utilize my skills outside of the classroom through volunteering. I volunteer at the local hospital, and volunteer for an organization called CRY, which supports Children's Rights in India. Having won many volunteer outreach awards and recognitions from them, I enjoy spending time helping others when im not stuck at home doing some math homework.
